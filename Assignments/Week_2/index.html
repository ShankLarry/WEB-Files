Overview

One of the most successful and important films of all time is 1993's Jurassic Park. The film is directed by Steven Spielberg and based off of a novel of the same name by Michael Chrichton. At the time of its release it was the highest grossing film of all time, and with it's later 3D rerelease became one of only a handful of films to gross over a billion dollars.

Plot

The film follows a group of outside experts who are brought in to give their assessment of a secret, unopened, theme park built on an island off the coast of Costa Rica by an eccentric billionaire mogul. That theme park turns out to be a "biological preserve" in which extinct dinosaurs have been cloned and brought back to life as an attraction. Due to a combination of human hubris, natural disaster, and poor hiring decisions, the power to the park's systems is shut down and the dinosaurs escape their enclosures, terrorizing those on the island.

Filming and Legacy

The film is historically significant as one of the first (though not the first) to embrace computer generated imagery (CGI) as a major component of its special effects. While many shots used practical effects, such as animatronics, much of the shots showing a full dinosaur were completely done using CGI.

The film became the start of a franchise that went on to spawn 6 sequels.
